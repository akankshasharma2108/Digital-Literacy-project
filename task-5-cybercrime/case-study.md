## **Part A – Case Study:Payment Fraud**

**Case Study: The Fake UPI Payment Link Scam**

UPI fraud is a type of cybercrime where criminals trick users into transferring money through fake payment links or apps. Typically, it starts with a message on WhatsApp or SMS claiming the victim has won a prize or owes a payment. The message contains a link to a fake UPI page that looks like a legitimate bank interface.

**Step-by-Step Scenario:**

1. The victim receives a message with a UPI payment request or reward claim.  
2. Clicking the link directs them to a fake bank interface.  
3. The victim enters their UPI PIN and account details.  
4. The fraudster immediately captures the information and transfers money from the victim’s account.

College students and young adults are often targeted because they frequently use UPI apps for small payments and may click links without verification.

**Consequences:**

Victims can lose thousands of rupees instantly. Recovery is difficult since UPI transactions are instant and irreversible. Beyond financial loss, victims often feel stressed and anxious, affecting their trust in digital payments.

*Source: Adapted from real incidents reported on National Cyber Crime Portal and media reports on UPI fraud in India.*
